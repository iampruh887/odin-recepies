VADA PAV



The heart of the show is a golden-fried fritter made with mashed potatoes, onions, spices like turmeric and asafoetida. Sometimes, green chillies add a bit of heat. The batter often uses chickpea flour (besan) for a crispy golden crust.


INGREDIENTS:

  • chutneys
  • masalasa
  • green chillies
  • potatoes

STEPS:

  1. Make the Batata Vada: In a large bowl, mix together mashed potatoes, onion, green chili, ginger, turmeric, red chili powder (optional), coriander powder, garam masala, hing, salt, and coriander leaves.
  2. Form the mixture into small balls, pressing them gently to flatten them slightly.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk besan with water to make a thick batter. Dip each vada ball in the batter, coating it evenly.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium heat. Fry the vada balls until golden brown and crispy on all sides. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Make the Chutneys: For the green chutney, grind together coriander leaves, mint leaves, green chilies, ginger, garlic, lemon juice, and salt. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.
  6. For the sweet chutney, soak tamarind pulp in warm water for 10 minutes. Strain the pulp and discard the seeds. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they crackle, add the tamarind pulp, jaggery/brown sugar, red chili powder, hing, and salt. Cook until the mixture thickens slightly.
  7. Assemble the Vada Pav: Slit open a pav and spread both green and sweet chutneys on the inside. Place a hot batata vada inside the pav, and top with dry garlic chutney, fried green chili, and chopped onion (optional).
  8. Enjoy your delicious homemade vada pav!
