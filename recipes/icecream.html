ICECREAM



There are endless possibilities when it comes to ice cream. From classic flavors like vanilla and chocolate to more adventurous options like lavender honey or avocado chip, there's something for everyone. And don't forget about all the different toppings and mix-ins! Hot fudge, sprinkles, whipped cream, and chopped nuts are just a few of the ways to take your ice cream to the next level.


INGREDIENTS

  • brown sugar
  • unsalted butter
  • vanilla extract
  • whole milk
  • eggs

STEPS:

  1. Make the butterscotch base: In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter and brown sugar together, stirring constantly until the sugar dissolves and the mixture becomes fragrant.
  2. Add the milk and vanilla extract: Whisk in the milk and vanilla extract, and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  3. Temper the egg yolks (if using): In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks. Slowly whisk in a small amount of the hot butterscotch mixture to the egg yolks to temper them. Gradually whisk the tempered egg yolks into the remaining hot butterscotch mixture.
  4. Thicken the base (if using): In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch with a tablespoon of the cold milk. Whisk the cornstarch slurry into the hot butterscotch mixture and cook, stirring constantly, until thickened.
  5. Chill the base: Remove the base from the heat and let it cool completely. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight.
  6. Churn the ice cream: Churn the chilled base in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  7. Fold in mix-ins (if using): Once the ice cream is churned, fold in any desired mix-ins, such as chopped nuts or butterscotch chips.
  8. Freeze: Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until firm.
